01
Itinerary One
Old Seoul
Seoul has two sides. South of Han River is modern and new. North of Han River is where the history is — old palaces, traditional markets, and remnants of the city wall.
Main Route
10 AM
Gyeongbokgung Palace
A large ancient palace in the center of the city, built in the 1300s. If you rent a hanbok (traditional Korean garment) nearby, entry is free — so you'll see a lot of people dressed up. Worth a couple of hours.
🚇 Subway or taxi
↗ Google Maps
Noon
Insadong-gil
A street near the palace with souvenir shops and small restaurants. Good place to pick up gifts. Walkable from Gyeongbokgung.
🚶 Walking from Gyeongbokgung
↗ Google Maps
2 PM
Gwangjang Market
One of Seoul's oldest traditional markets. Busy and full of street food stalls. Good spot for a late lunch — bindaetteok (mung bean pancake) and mayak gimbap are the things to get here.
🚶 ~25 min walk or subway
↗ Google Maps
4 PM
Cheonggye-cheon Waterway
A stream running through old Seoul, converted into a walking path. A pleasant way to walk between spots and decompress after the market.
🚶 Right next to the market
↗ Google Maps
5 PM
Dongdaemun Fortress — Sunset Walk
Parts of Seoul's old fortress wall have been restored into a trail. "Dongdaemun" means the East Gate — one of the four original entrances to the old city. Walk up the hill for a view of the city at sunset. Small pubs at the top.
🚶 Walk along the waterway or Uber
↗ Google Maps
Nearby — If You Have Time
Euljiro (을지로)
Old industrial printing district that's become a bar area. Unpretentious, local crowd, cheap beer.
↗ Google Maps
Ikseondong (익선동)
Small alley of hanok buildings turned into cafes and shops. 10-min walk from Insadong.

02
Itinerary Two
Han River South
A Buddhist temple next to skyscrapers, a massive underground mall, and instant ramyeon by the river.
Main Route
10 AM
Bongeunsa Temple (봉은사)
A Buddhist temple that's been here since the 9th century, now surrounded by Gangnam skyscrapers. The contrast is striking. Quiet inside, free to enter.
🚇 Subway to Samseong station
↗ Google Maps
Noon
COEX Mall & Starfield Library
One of the largest underground malls in Asia. The Starfield Library inside is worth seeing — a large open atrium lined floor-to-ceiling with bookshelves. Plenty of places to eat lunch here.
🚶 5-min walk from Bongeunsa
↗ Google Maps
4 PM
Banpo Han River Park — Ramyeon
Buy instant ramyeon from a GS25 or CU convenience store at the park — they have hot water dispensers and will give you chopsticks. Sit by the water. Simple but one of those experiences that's very specific to Seoul.
🚇 Subway to Express Bus Terminal, then short walk
↗ Google Maps
Nearby — If You Have Time
Dosan Park Area (도산공원)
Tree-lined streets, good independent restaurants and cafes. A quieter, more local side of Gangnam.
↗ Google Maps
Seongsu-dong (성수동)
Former factory district turned café and concept store neighborhood. Popular on weekends.

03
Itinerary Three
Myeongdong · Namsan · Itaewon
A big museum in the morning, street food in Myeongdong, up to the tower in the middle of the city, then down into the most international neighborhood for dinner.
Main Route
10 AM
National Museum of Korea
Free entry, one of the largest museums in the world. Covers Korean history from prehistoric times through the Joseon dynasty. Don't try to see everything — the gold crowns, celadon pottery, and Buddhist sculpture hall are the highlights. Plan for about 2 hours.
🚇 Subway to Ichon station
💰 Free entry
↗ Google Maps
1 PM
Myeongdong
Seoul's most well-known shopping and street food district. It's touristy but the street food is good — try the egg bread (계란빵), skewers, and hotteok. A good spot for lunch before heading up to Namsan.
🚇 Subway to Myeongdong station
↗ Google Maps
3 PM
Namsan Tower
A tower on a small mountain in the middle of Seoul. Take the cable car up from Myeongdong or hike (about 20 minutes). The observation deck has a full view of the city. Better in the late afternoon when the light is good.
🚡 Cable car from Myeongdong
↗ Google Maps
6 PM
Itaewon — Dinner & Nightlife
Come down from Namsan into Itaewon for dinner. Seoul's most international neighborhood — more variety of food here than anywhere else in the city. Middle Eastern, Mexican, South Asian, and more. Also one of the better areas in Seoul for nightlife if you want to stay out.
🚶 Short walk or taxi down from Namsan
↗ Google Maps
Nearby — If You Have Time
Haebangchon (해방촌)
Hillside neighborhood just above Itaewon. Quieter, more local feel. Good cafes and small bars.
↗ Google Maps
Gyeongnidan-gil (경리단길)
Restaurant street between Itaewon and Namsan. Compact and walkable, good food options.

Beyond the Itineraries
Other Recommendations
Standalone spots, nature, and a few ideas for when you want to get out of the city.
Worth Knowing About
Landmark
Lotte World Tower
The tallest building in Korea. The observation deck on the 117th floor (Seoul Sky) has views that stretch far beyond the city on a clear day. Worth going if you want a different perspective from Namsan. There's a mall and an amusement park attached if you want to make a full day of it.
🚇 Subway to Jamsil station
↗ Google Maps
Traditional
Bukchon Hanok Village
A residential neighborhood of traditional hanok houses on a hillside between two palaces. Most of old Seoul was demolished during modernization, so this is one of the few areas where the original architecture survived. It's a real neighborhood, so keep noise down.
🚇 Subway to Anguk station
↗ Google Maps
Nature
Bukhansan & Inwangsan
Seoul has proper mountains on its edge, both accessible by subway. Bukhansan is the bigger hike — granite peaks, 2 to 3 hours. Inwangsan is shorter and easier, about an hour up, passing along part of the old city wall with good views. Both are free.
🚇 Bukhansan: Gupabal or Ui station · Inwangsan: Dongnimmun station
↗ Bukhansan — Google Maps ↗ Inwangsan — Google Maps
Day Trip
Busan
If you have a spare day and want to get out of Seoul, Busan is worth it. Korea's second largest city on the southern coast — beaches, seafood markets, a completely different pace. The KTX from Seoul Station gets you there in about 2.5 hours. Doable as a long day trip, more comfortable as an overnight.
🚄 KTX from Seoul Station (~2.5 hrs)
